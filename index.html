About Me

I hold a PhD in Psychology (Cognitive and Brain Sciences) from the University of Minnesota, where I conducted empirical research on auditory perception and music cognition. My doctoral dissertation explored voice segregation cues in polyphonic music perception across both normal and impaired hearing populations, defended in April 2026.

With a background spanning clinical psychology, musicology, and quantitative research, I bring a multidisciplinary perspective to complex research questions. I specialize in designing rigorous experiments, managing large behavioral datasets, and communicating findings to both technical and non-technical audiences through clear visualizations and compelling narratives.
